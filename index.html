WHYTEGOD

A Public, Read-Only Crypto Reference Protocol

What This Is

Whytegod is a read-only public protocol that explains what crypto systems actually do — before trust is required, before value is transferred, before risk is taken.

It does not execute. It does not custody. It does not persuade.

What Whytegod Does

  • Translates crypto actions into plain human language
  • Separates mathematical certainty from human assumptions
  • Reveals control, dependency, and failure points
  • Applies the same logic to all systems equally

First Verifiable Output Example

User Input:
“I want to stake Token X on Platform Y for 20% APY.”

What Will Happen

  • You send Token X to a smart contract controlled by Platform Y
  • Your tokens are locked under rules you cannot change

What Must Be True

  • The platform must generate or redistribute funds
  • The contract must remain unchanged
  • The operators must continue acting honestly

What Might Happen

  • Rewards may reduce or stop
  • Withdrawal conditions may change
  • Contracts may be upgraded or replaced

What Cannot Happen

  • Guaranteed returns
  • Risk-free yield
  • Profit without human dependency
Plain Language Translation:
“You are trusting people and code you cannot control. The 20% is conditional, not mathematical.”

Input Grammar v0.1

Whytegod accepts only inputs that describe a specific crypto action. If an action cannot be described clearly, it cannot be analyzed truthfully.

Required Structure:
ACTION + ASSET + PLATFORM + PROMISE (optional)
Example:
Stake ETH on Lido for 5% APY

Allowed Actions

  • Buy
  • Sell
  • Stake
  • Lock
  • Bridge
  • Swap
  • Lend
  • Borrow
  • Mint
  • Provide Liquidity
  • Delegate

Rules

  • Assets must be named
  • Platforms must be identified
  • Promises are analyzed, never trusted
  • Predictions and opinions are rejected

Invalid Inputs

Will I make money? This is a good project Explain crypto

What Whytegod Does Not Do

  • No custody
  • No execution
  • No predictions
  • No promotions
  • No financial advice
Notice:
Whytegod does not tell you what to do. It shows you what is true.
