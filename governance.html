What governance means here

Governance in Whytegod is not about voting every week or chasing consensus.

It is about **limiting power** before distributing it.

Protocol first, people second

The protocol defines the rules.

Humans interpret, maintain, and improve — but do not override core guarantees.

Minimal governance surface

Fewer levers mean fewer attacks.

Whytegod deliberately limits the number of parameters that can be changed.

Slow upgrades

No emergency upgrades. No midnight changes.

Changes follow: proposal → review → cooling period → execution.

Public reasoning

Decisions must be explained in human language.

Silence is not governance.

No permanent rulers

Roles are temporary.

Authority decays unless renewed transparently.

Forking is allowed

The ultimate governance right is the right to leave.

Whytegod does not trap users or developers.

Governance is restraint

The goal is not to rule faster.

The goal is to rule less.

Whytegod governance exists to prevent abuse — not to enable control.
