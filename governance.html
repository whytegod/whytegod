Governance

Governance exists only where the protocol is incomplete.

Protocol First

Whytegod prioritizes protocol rules over human discretion.

Where behavior can be expressed in code, it is removed from governance.

No Central Authority

There are no administrators, operators, or executives.

No individual or group can override protocol behavior.

No Continuous Voting

Constant voting creates instability.

Whytegod avoids perpetual proposals, campaigns, and influence accumulation.

Change Is Slow by Design

Modifications require clear necessity, technical justification, and broad consensus.

Friction is intentional.

Forking Is the Ultimate Check

If disagreement cannot be resolved, the protocol may be forked.

Exit is preferred over control.

No Governance Tokens

Influence is not purchased.

Tokens do not grant authority.

Documentation Over Debate

Decisions are justified through reasoning, not popularity.

Records matter more than rhetoric.

Role of Contributors

Contributors propose changes.

The protocol accepts only what survives scrutiny.

Conclusion

Governance is not a feature.

It is a temporary necessity on the path to determinism.
