Privacy

Privacy is not a feature of Whytegod. It is a constraint.

Principle

Whytegod minimizes knowledge. What is not collected cannot be leaked, sold, requested, or abused.

What We Do Not Collect

  • No personal identity information
  • No names, emails, or phone numbers
  • No IP tracking or fingerprinting
  • No behavioral analytics
  • No cookies for profiling

If data is not required for the system to function, it is excluded by design.

What the Protocol Can See

The protocol observes only what is mathematically necessary:

  • Valid inputs
  • Valid state transitions
  • Deterministic outputs

It does not interpret meaning. It does not infer intent.

No Surveillance Model

Whytegod does not optimize engagement, retention, or user behavior.

  • No dashboards watching users
  • No metrics measuring attention
  • No incentives to extract data

Observation creates power. Power creates abuse.

Off-Chain Interaction

Any off-chain tools or interfaces are optional and replaceable.

They should be assumed untrusted. Privacy depends on the protocol, not the interface.

Responsibility of the User

Privacy is preserved by default, but cannot protect against voluntary disclosure.

  • Do not reuse identities.
  • Do not link wallets unnecessarily.
  • Assume public data is permanent.

No Privacy Promises

Whytegod does not promise anonymity.

It promises restraint.

The system is designed to know as little as possible, forever.

Closing

Privacy is not secrecy. It is silence.

Whytegod stays silent by design.
