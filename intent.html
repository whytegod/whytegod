Intent

Intent is a declarative expression of a desired financial outcome.

In Whytegod, users do not specify routes, platforms, or counterparties. They specify what must be true after execution.

Example

Not: “Swap token A for token B on exchange X.”

But: “End with exposure to asset B within constraints Y.”

Why Intent Matters

  • Removes platform dependency
  • Enables execution competition
  • Separates user goals from system mechanics
  • Reduces cognitive and trust load

Intent is immutable. Execution is replaceable.
