Intent

Intent is the core abstraction of the Whytegod Protocol. It represents what a user wants to achieve, without specifying how execution must occur.

In traditional financial systems, users are forced to manually control routing, timing, intermediaries, and trust assumptions. Whytegod removes this burden by making intent the primary input.

What Is an Intent?

An intent is a declarative statement of outcome. It describes a desired result, not an execution path.

  • No exchange selection
  • No routing logic
  • No counterparty negotiation
  • No custody requirements
Example intent:

“I want to transfer value to another party securely, without revealing unnecessary information, and without relying on a centralized intermediary.”

Why Intent Matters

Intent-based systems scale human decision-making while allowing machines to handle complexity.

  • Humans define goals
  • Protocols enforce rules
  • Executors compete to fulfill intents
  • No single entity controls execution

Intent in Whytegod

Whytegod treats intent as a first-class protocol object. It can be validated, signed, relayed, and executed without exposing users to execution-layer risk.

This separation enables open participation, composability, and long-term decentralization.
