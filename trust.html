Trust

Whytegod does not request trust.

Trust Is a Liability

Systems that require trust introduce dependency.

Dependency introduces failure.

Removal of Authority

Whytegod has no operators, administrators, or privileged actors.

There is no party whose honesty must be assumed.

Deterministic Behavior

The protocol behaves the same way regardless of who interacts with it.

Identity is irrelevant.

No Custody

Whytegod does not hold assets, keys, data, or user state.

What is not held cannot be stolen.

No Hidden Incentives

The protocol does not benefit from user behavior.

There is no revenue dependency embedded into its operation.

Open Interpretation

Documentation exists to be read, questioned, and challenged.

Nothing relies on belief.

Failure Is Allowed

The protocol makes no promise of success.

Systems that admit failure do not need trust.

Conclusion

Trust is not established here.

It is rendered unnecessary.
